Self-discipline

The ability to control one's feelings and overcome one's weaknesses; the ability to pursue what one thinks is right despite temptations to abandon it.

How to implement self-discipline

Self-discipline is built on competence, persistence, and the willingness to assume accountability for results. Competence is more than skill and ability; it's attitude and experience. Get the right people involved, and self-discipline comes more easily. There is no binary switch to go from no discipline to self-discipline; it's a journey that some individuals get right away and some take longer to achieve. Growing and maturing effective agile teams takes time and dedication to build relationships and encouraging collaboration. This is the most important task of the project leader and individual team members also.

Why is self-discipline important

Self-dicipline is one of the most important parts about building a team. It enables freedom and empowerment. One of the most acute dangers of process-centric development and project management is that they reduce any incentive for self-dicipline. Self-diciplined teams accomplish near-miraculous things.
